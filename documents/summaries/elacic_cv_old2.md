# Gone in 30 Days! Predictions for Car Import Planning

Forecasting demand in the automotive import business is its own set of headaches. Our study looks at how well various machine learning methods can predict the number of cars to be imported on a monthly basis—a non-trivial task, given noisy real-world market data and volatile consumer behaviour.

We approached the problem with multiple predictive algorithms, including linear regression and more advanced ensemble methods. The models were trained on historical data from actual import records, accounting for time trends and seasonal effects.

One of the key results: ensemble models provided notably higher forecasting accuracy compared to baseline single-model approaches. Still, predicting market shifts remains challenging—unexpected spikes and dips can push error rates up, regardless of tuning. Traditional linear approaches lagged in responsiveness, while tree-based models adapted better to sudden changes in demand patterns.

From a practical perspective, the work demonstrates how data-driven forecasting tools can support automotive importers in logistical planning and inventory management. Yet, the unavoidable uncertainty of the market means the human element in planning can’t be fully replaced by automatable predictions, at least not yet.
