# elacic_cv.pdf

This is my academic curriculum vitae rather than a traditional research paper, but it provides a snapshot of my scholarly work, professional experience, and community service. The document outlines my progression from undergraduate studies in software engineering and information systems at the University of Zagreb, through a PhD in Computer Science at Graz University of Technology, up to my current position as Principal Engineer at Infobip.

The portfolio highlights both my research in recommender systems—particularly work on fairness, bias mitigation, session-based recommendations, and the integration of embeddings—as well as significant collaborative efforts and industry projects. My involvement spans editorial duties (Frontiers in Big Data), program committee memberships for ACM conferences, and direct mentorship through thesis supervision.

If you are seeking a detailed account of my academic output, including key contributions to recommender systems and trustworthy AI, this CV lists the major peer-reviewed publications, recognitions, and grants. It also includes service to the scientific community, such as organizing workshops and chairing conference tracks. For potential collaborators and students, this is the most direct overview of my research trajectory and current interests. Honestly, it’s less a narrative exploration and more a fact-rich ledger—but if you want context on the projects, drop me a line.
